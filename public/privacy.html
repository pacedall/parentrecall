Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2026

ParentRecall is built to be private by design. It is a personal memory aid — not a social network. We don’t sell your data, we don’t show ads, and we never store photographs of children. This policy explains what we collect, why, and the control you have over it.

1. Who we are

ParentRecall is a service operated by Pacedall Labs Ltd (“we”, “us”), a company registered in England and Wales (company number 16619412), registered office 71–75 Shelton Street, Covent Garden, London, WC2H 9JQ. We are the data controller for the personal information described here, and we are registered with the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) under reference ZB969483 (registered 27 August 2025).

For any privacy question or request, contact us at team@parentrecall.com.

2. The information we collect

Account information

When you create an account we collect your email address, a password (which we store only as a securely hashed value — we never see or store your actual password), and optionally a first name you choose to give.

The information you add

ParentRecall stores the information you enter to help you remember people, namely: your children’s first names; the clubs and classes you set up; and, for each person you want to remember, their name (with surnames automatically shortened — see below), an optional description of who they are, optional parent/carer names, your own free-text memory notes, an optional birthday, and a cartoon avatar you build.

We never collect or store photographs. There is no image upload anywhere in the product; faces are illustrated avatars generated from the options you pick.

Please don’t record sensitive information

Your memory notes are free text. We ask that you do not enter special-category information — such as health, medical, religious, ethnic, sexual, or similar sensitive details — about yourself or anyone else. ParentRecall is a name-and-face memory aid and does not need it. If you choose to record such details, you do so for your own personal purposes and are responsible for that choice; we store the note only to provide the service to you and never use it for any other purpose.

Technical information

To keep the service secure and working we process limited technical data such as your IP address and request logs (for example to apply rate limits and detect abuse).

3. Information about other people

By its nature, ParentRecall lets you record limited details about other people — classmates, fellow parents, and coaches. We’ve designed the product to minimise this:

  • Surnames are automatically shortened to two letters (for example “John Smith” is stored as “John Sm”).
  • No photographs of anyone are ever stored.
  • Your lists are private to your household — there is no sharing, no directory, and no way for other users to find them.

You should only record information you have a genuine personal reason to remember, and only what is necessary for that purpose. You can edit or delete any person’s details at any time, and deleting your account removes all of it.

4. How we use your information, and our lawful bases

  • To provide the service — creating your account and storing the lists you build (lawful basis: performance of our contract with you).
  • To send necessary account emails — email verification, password resets, and partner invitations (performance of our contract).
  • To send optional birthday reminders, which you can turn off (lawful basis: our legitimate interest in a useful service, and your control over them).
  • To keep the service secure and reliable — preventing abuse, debugging, and protecting accounts (legitimate interests).

5. Email

We use Resend to deliver transactional emails (verification, password reset, partner invites, and optional reminders). We do not send marketing emails.

6. Who we share information with

We do not sell your personal information and we do not use it for advertising. We share it only with the service providers that help us run ParentRecall, and only as needed:

  • Railway — cloud hosting and database.
  • Resend — sending the emails described above.
  • Cloudflare — DNS, security, and content delivery.
  • Zoho — our email inbox, used only if you contact us at team@parentrecall.com.

We may also disclose information if required by law, or to protect our rights, users, or the public.

7. International transfers

Our hosting and database provider (Railway) stores and processes data on servers in the United States, and some other providers (such as Resend and Cloudflare) are also based outside the UK. Where personal data is transferred internationally, we rely on appropriate safeguards — such as the UK International Data Transfer Agreement, the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, or equivalent contractual protections — to keep it protected to UK standards.

8. How long we keep it

We keep your account and the information you add for as long as your account is open. When you delete your account (or a household admin deletes the household), we permanently remove the data from our live systems. Residual copies held in encrypted backups are overwritten on our normal backup cycle, within 30 days. After deletion we keep a minimal deletion record — the account email address and the dates the account was created, last accessed and deleted — for security, abuse-prevention, and to evidence that deletion requests were honoured. It contains no other personal information about you.

9. How we protect it

Connections are encrypted in transit (HTTPS). Passwords are hashed with bcrypt. Access to your data requires a signed authentication token, and each household’s data is isolated so one account cannot read another’s. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

10. Family (household) accounts

If you invite a partner to your account, they receive their own sign-in and can see and edit the same family lists. The account admin can remove the partner at any time; doing so revokes their access but keeps the shared information. If you are added as a partner, the admin of that household can see the information in it.

11. Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to our use of your personal information, and the right to data portability. You can exercise the main ones directly in the app: Account → Export my data downloads everything, and Delete account erases it. For a step-by-step guide (including how to request deletion if you can’t sign in), see parentrecall.com/delete-account. For any other request, email team@parentrecall.com.

If you have a concern we can’t resolve, you can complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

12. Children

ParentRecall is intended for adults (parents and guardians) aged 18 or over. It is not directed at children and we do not knowingly allow children to create accounts. Information about children that appears in the app is entered by their parent or guardian for their own personal use.

13. Cookies and local storage

We don’t use tracking or advertising cookies. To keep you signed in, the app stores a sign-in token in your browser. If you tick “Keep me signed in on this device” when logging in, it is kept in your browser’s local storage so you stay signed in between visits; if you leave it unticked, it is kept only for the current session and cleared when you close the browser. A small amount of other data may also be stored locally. This all stays on your device, and signing out clears the sign-in token.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes we’ll update the date at the top and, where appropriate, let you know in the app or by email.
